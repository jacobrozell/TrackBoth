TrackBoth ("the app") is a habit and vice tracker published by Jacob Rozell. This policy describes what data the app handles and your choices.

Data we store on your device

The app stores habit names, daily log entries (completion status, optional notes, motivations, and quantities), and goals locally on your iPhone or iPad using Apple's on-device storage (SwiftData). This data stays on your device unless you choose to export or back it up.

iCloud backup (optional)

If you tap Backup to iCloud or Restore from iCloud in Settings, your habit data is stored in your private iCloud account via Apple CloudKit. TrackBoth does not operate its own servers for this data. Apple's iCloud terms and privacy policy apply.

Export and import

You may export your data as a JSON file and share it through the system share sheet, or import a JSON file you previously exported. Export/import is initiated by you; the app does not upload exports automatically.

Data we do not collect

  • No account registration with TrackBoth
  • No advertising or ad identifiers
  • No sale of personal information
  • No third-party analytics SDKs in lean 1.0

Diagnostics

In development builds, debug logs may be written to the system console. Release builds use Apple's unified logging (OSLog) for error diagnostics only and do not include habit content in logs sent off-device.

Children

The app is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Your choices

  • Reset all local data — Settings → Reset All Local Data removes local habits and entries.
  • Stop using iCloud — simply do not use backup/restore; delete iCloud backups from your Apple ID settings if needed.

Changes

We may update this policy. The effective date at the top will change when we do. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance.
